3 Introduction

Hello and welcome to The Biodiversity and Landscape Ecology Research Facility (BLERF) Lab at UBC’s Okanagan campus!

This lab handbook is a living document that sets out our community guidelines, values, shared principles, and expectations. If you’re here because you’re curious about the lab (woohoo!), the handbook will give you a sense of who we are and whether our research community is one you’d like to become part of. All new BLERF members should read the handbook upon joining the lab.

4 Territory, Acknowledgements, Support

We respectfully acknowledge that we live and work on the traditional and unceded territory of Syilx Okanagan people. 5 Areas of Research

Here in the BLERF lab, our research often spans many disciplines and areas of research. Below we have listed a few of our current interests. However, we are always open to exploring new areas of research!

  • The Historical/biogeographical perspectives on associations between plants and mycorrhizal fungi
  • Landscape connectivity and biodiversity conservation
  • Historical and biogeographical influences over local communities
  • Invasive species ecology and biocontrol
  • Increasing the openness and transparency of scientific research via the open science movement

10 Code of Conduct

Diversity is a fact

Just like life is diverse, so are we. Everyone is welcomed independently of their culture, religion, race and colour, sexual orientation, gender identity, differing abilities, and mental health status, personal and academic background, age, economic status or body appearance.

Inclusion is an act

  • Use the preferred pronouns if you know someone. Otherwise, prefer a gender-neutral language.
  • Put an effort in correctly pronouncing people’s names. There is no shame in mispronouncing names, as long as you try your best.
  • Allow personal space, and respect others’ privacy. Be self-conscious of your language and behaviour.
  • Develop a land acknowledgment and identify your positionality (you can consult this UBC resource)
  • If you realize you’ve erred, apologize quickly and move forward.
  • But most importantly, be kind.

Equity is the outcome

We hope to create a mutually supportive community, a safe and inclusive environment, a place where you can feel at home. You are valued, and your ideas and work are valued.

Other key considerations:

  • Inclusion/accessibility during meetings (lab and others as needed)
    • closed captioning for talks
    • varied ways to participate
    • accommodating dietary restrictions and preferences
    • other
  • Health & safety
  • Ethics
  • Sustainability
  • Professionalism

There exists many other channels and sub-channels where students can ask project-related questions, share updates, documents, etc.

  • Tap on “Add Channel” to browse existing channels you may not have been added to, or to create a new channel. DM groups can also be created with up to 8 individuals.

12 Meetings

12.1 Lab Meetings

  • During the school year, the BLERF lab hosts a weekly lab meeting. These lab meetings are typically 60 minutes and cover a range of topics. The meeting schedule can be found at the BLERF OSF.

  • During the summer, these lab meetings typically become more sporadic as lab members are away on vacation or busy with fieldwork.

  • Often students and faculty from other labs will join these weekly meetings.

12.2 One-on-one Meetings

  • Apart from the weekly lab meeting, members of the BLERF lab usually have individual weekly meetings with Jason.

  • Students are expected to generate an agenda in advance of these meetings. This agenda helps ensure that we make the most of our meeting time.

  • Meeting agendas and notes should be posted on OSF for accountability (for both student and supervisor)

19 Authorship Conventions

A very general guideline is that authorship is warranted if a person contributes to at least two of these:

  • Designing the study.
  • Acquiring the data.
  • Analyzing the data.
  • Writing the paper.

All authors must agree on what is finally submitted for publication, and must agree to be fully accountable for what is submitted.

However, the most important advice is that authorship should be discussed at the outset of any collaboration.

20 Data Ownership Conventions

In general, data collected as part of BLERF activities will be archived on Dataverse, and publicly accessible (sometimes after a suitable embargo period).

The primary collectors of the data (e.g. graduate students) are, by default, expected to maintain rights to the data, as will the primary advisor (Dr. Pither), who will be the steward of the data unless otherwise arranged at the outset of the collaboration.

Exceptions to these general policies include:

  • Data collected as part of partnerships with Indigenous partners, who will decide if, how, and by whom data will be archived and accessed, following OCAP Principles.
  • Sensitive data of any sort, which will be managed according to appropriate jurisdictional guidelines.

Please also consult UBC’s guidelines about authorship and data ownership. That same webpage has lots of other good resources!
